These four statuses are the most important distinctions to understand when tracking a petition through the SOS website.

Filed
The sample petition sheet has been submitted to the SOS. The petition is posted publicly but cannot yet collect signatures. The SOS, AG, and State Auditor are actively reviewing it.
Approved for Circulation
The Official Ballot Title has been certified. The petition can now be actively circulated and signatures legally collected. This is the first major green light — but the measure is NOT yet on the ballot.
Submitted
The petitioner has delivered all signed petition pages to the SOS by the deadline. Signature verification by local election authorities begins.
Certificate of Sufficiency
The SOS has verified that sufficient valid signatures exist. The measure is officially certified for the November ballot. This is the definitive "on the ballot" status.
⚠️ Common Confusion
"Approved for Circulation" does not mean the measure is on the ballot. It means signature collection can begin. A petition approved for circulation may never gather enough signatures to qualify. The measure is only definitively on the ballot once a Certificate of Sufficiency is issued.

Referendum petitions follow most of the same process as initiative petitions, but with these key differences:

Purpose
Allows citizens to refer a law already passed by the General Assembly back to voters for approval or rejection.
Filing Deadline
Referendum petition must be filed with the SOS within 90 days of the final adjournment of the GA session that passed the law.
Sig. Threshold
Same as statutory initiative: 5% of legal voters in any 6 of 8 congressional districts.
Exemptions
Laws necessary for immediate public peace, health, or safety — and current appropriations bills — cannot be referred by petition.
GA Referral
The General Assembly itself can also refer legislation to voters without any petition.
Governor's Veto
The governor's veto power does not apply to measures referred to the people.
Note on Shared Process
Once a referendum petition clears the filing and form-approval steps, the signature collection, submission, verification, and Certificate of Sufficiency steps work identically to the initiative petition process.
